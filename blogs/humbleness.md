---
title: Another argument for being humble
date: 2026-05-16
---

When we become aware of another person’s assumptions of us it unconciously places expectations upon ourselves to fufill that expectation. Our identity then becomes reinforced to live up to that idea, perhaps causing us to change how we act in class, treating other people, or who we choose to associate ourselves with.

Being humble is the antidote. By realizing that the differences between people are quite miniscule, we become aware that awards and achievments are friviolous in the face of life, death, and happiness. It loosens that burden to live up to a certain role allowing us to freely examine ideas, actions, and most importantly take risks. 

When we are stuck in a box of expectations we stagnate. Action, risk, and exploration are the methods to grow our minds and perspectives.
