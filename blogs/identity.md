*you are the sum of the 5 closest people around you*

This is a popular maxim online, and one I find to have a lot of merit. Alexandr Wang was roomates with Sam Altman during the Covid 19 pandemic. Alexander the Great was taught be Aristotle. The Beatles in Hamburg, who created the most talented muscians of the era but were small time schoolboys before that. The PayPal mafia includes Peter Thiel, Elon Musk, Reid Hoffman, Max Levchin, and Roelof Botha. The Lost Generation with Ernest Hemmingway's Paris circle included Gertrude Stein, F. Scott Fitzgeerald, Ezra Pound, and James Joyce. The point is great people in certain fields seem to tightly hold onto one another.

What I believe is the reason that underlies this effect of people is identity. When a person chooses to spend time with another person, it reinforces their identity. Every single person has a perception about them that creates their *reputation*. That reputation is essential because it attracts or repulses people. That gravitaitonal effect is due to values. People with similar values tend to gravitate towards one another. When a person chooses their closest friends, partners, or mentors they are inadvertently using their *reputation* as a way to measure if they share similar values. When a person is accepted by that individual or group it is a reinforcment of their identity.

What I believe is that identity is the underlying force that drives all action. All action comes from some fufillment of that identity, which stems from certain needs and wants. (I'll expand more in another article)

This is why choosing the right person to spend time with is essential. If they are not in *alignment* with your values then they will feel like a sinking weight. However, a person that reinforces your strongest values will strengthen your identity allowing you to flourish.
