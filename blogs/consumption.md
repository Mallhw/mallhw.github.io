While scrolling through a [Harvard article]((https://www.health.harvard.edu/blog/nutritional-psychiatry-your-brain-on-food-201511168626)) that found that some Mediterranean or Japanese diets can decrease depression by 25% or 30%.

It got me thinking about the idea of *consumption* and how it can be used as a lens to see our choices and actions. I've personally been pretty picky about food as a whole. I'm strictly off soda, generally off sweets, and reduced simple carbohydrate consumption overall. And, I feel better, happier, and healthier as a result. 

When we hear the word consumption it's often in connatation with food. It's pervaded our entire society, just think how big of brands McDonalds or Coco-Cola are in American society. For the first time in history, since 2012-2013, there are more cases of obesity than malnourishment. That represents a fundamental shift in society: there are too many options. The consumerist, capitalist society has simply just produced far more opprutunities, products, and options than ever before. Instead of worrying about is there food? The quesiton now is what cuisine are you feeling? That's a departure from our ancestors in a society that used to be all about scarcity. Our homo-sapien brains were not evolved for a world of abudance, so now the paradigm of our natural biology must be ignored. 

Consumption is my methodology for how to approach the glitz and glamour of the 21st century. What kind of foods do we eat? When do you know its not healthy for you? These are most tanigablly seen in food but that's not all consumption can be. Consumption can also be our use of social media - doomscrolling, brainrotting, thoughtless mindless activities. The information that is consumed impacts our brain the same way that food does. 

This points to one underlying quality: the environment impacts our mood, ideas, and health. There is a certain tension between the relationship of self vs the environment. How much do we control our enviromnets? How much does our environment control ourselves? In a world where ideas are built overnight, products are advertised relentlessly, and the mundane has been sensentionalized the examination of consumption helps bring power back towards the self. What we consume we inevitablly change us. But. We can choose what we consume.
