title: humans are irrational
date: 2026-05-12
---

Human emotion is irrational. It's been 68 days since I broke up with my ex girlfriend, my first one, and I've been undergoing reflection about how that relationship has changed me. Every single day since that, I've always had some passing thought about the relationship, not always in a mournful way but moreso an interesting curiosity. 

Rationally, I have the concious belief that this shouldn't particularily matter. I knew heading into the relationship that I most likely wasn't going to find the one, around 1-2% of highschool relationships end up in a marrige without divorce. I was also too young to truly know who I was or what I liked. Even during the relationship, I realized subconciously that this wasn't my person. Yet an underlying driver of myself is the striving for excellence, and I pursued the idea of being the absolute *best* boyfriend I possibly could. 

When we broke up, I experienced multitude of emotions. The one I didn't experience was regret becuase I believed I did the absolute best I could. That was an intrinsic philsophy of mine: to push oneself to be the best.

Anyways, my reflection on this has led me to happily reject one of the theories that was bothering me. While learning AP Macro/Micro Economics this year, I came to the disturbing notion that all human behavior was a transactinoal relationship to maximize their own happiness. Were all humans machine learning models that performed gradient ascent until they reached a local maximum of happiness? I believed this to be false. What I've come to theorize is that the aggregate of many, many humans - ranging to the millions - may show signs of rationality but individual relationships are marred with irrationality. Recently, I saw some behavior that made me question the rationality of people. It's made me realize that many psychological dispositions - such as avoidant anxious behaviors, Sunk Cost Fallacy, Stockholm Syndrome, procrastination, Endowment effect, Placebo effect, etc - are examples of irrational behavior among humans. However, there are actions that lean more rational such as buying groceries that are cheaper when comparing one against the other or deciding to avoid a stranger who is rude. What I believe breaks down rationality is emotion, the underlying cause behind all irrational behavior. The stronger the emotion, the less rational a person becomes. It's an inverse relationship. 

That's why economics is not a good framework to view individualized relationships. People simply don't care... or maybe just care too much.
