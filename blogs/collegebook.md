I'm going to summarize this book I saw in a bookstore for $8. I bought it on a whim and have been reading it on the train ride back. The book involves a study on hundreds of interviews on Harvard students.

Most interesting points
- Try to get to know 1 professors every semester really well. That will result in around 8 professors who be vital towards the academic rich experience
- Writing is the number one most appreciated subject to improve upon
- Clear relationship between participation on campus (volunteering, clubs, ecs, research, athletics) and satisfaction in college. 4/5 students said a *critical* moment in development happened outside of the classroom.
  - Apparently athletes are one of the happiest students
- Connect academics/what you study to something you care about outside of school will radically change career focus
