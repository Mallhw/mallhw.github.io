Some of my favorite Paul Grahm essays are those that concern writing. Paul is probably my favorite internet writer for many reasons, but what strikes me the most is the clarity of his thought - or *intention* as I would like to say. There is a clear reasoning for ever sentence, ones that turn, spin, stop, and play around with my thoughts. I love reading his work because its very obvious where his line of reasoning and thought come from.

So to commenmorate some of my favorite parts of his work, I want to talk about his ideas about writing. More specifically, the importance of writing. Because Paul Grahm has a very strong belief that good writing means clear thinking, and I also believe that. Because unlike speech, writing is permanent. Words linger for seconds in the air before disappearing. However, writing is engraved onto the text, meaning it can face far more scrutiny. Thus, writers have to be absolutely sure on what they are saying and that's what leads to clear thinking. Writers **must** be clear thinkers otherwise their words jamble up and become incoherent. There is minimal coverage for bad writing.  Because of this, writers are forced to be more attentive to their train of thought. 

I've noticed some effects of clear writing in helping determine the direction I want to take in not only writing but also conversation.
